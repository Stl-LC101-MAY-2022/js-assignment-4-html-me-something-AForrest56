Been there. Done that. Can we go back?

In November of 2020 one of my sisters had planned to compete in the Disney Wine and Dine Half Marathon Weekend. She had planned to take a weekend trip to Florida to compete in the race. She already had the hotel figured out, the tickets for the race purchased and was just waiting to get the plane tickets.
Enter Covid-19!
Disney unfortunately cancelled the races for the health and safety of all people involved. My sister was left with a huge decision to make. Cancel a weekend Disney trip? That is not how this family rolls. Instead her and our mother got on the phone with the Disney travel employees to come to an agreement.
When they called to ask if they could still visit, they were told about the hotel being shut down during that time. Since less people would be traveling due to the virus risk, Disney had plans to refurbish some parts of their property (a.k.a. the hotel!). Disney does there best to help with guest experiences and they did not disappoint. They offered my mother and sister an upgraded hotel for the same dates and the same price! They also let them add 2 days to the stay as well as add a few more people. WOOHOO! Disney Family Vacation!
Our travel plans were set! Mom, 4 daughters and 1 grand-daughter are going to Disney! From November 5th to the 9th we would all stay at Disney's Animal Kingdom Villas-Kidani Village.

Transportation Station

The travel time to Disney is always the worst part. Don't get me wrong road trips can be fun, but 14+ hours is a long time. We normally drive to Florida from Missouri. I know, I know. That's a long drive. Why not fly? Well, for 6 or more people it is cheaper to drive. Unfortunately, this time, we did not have the time to spare for driving such a long distance. We decided to take a flight from Lambert Airport to Orlando's Airport the morning of November 5th! Did I mention that is the day after my birthday. Lucky me right?
I am not fond of flying, but the airport was nice. We had to wake up quite early to jump on a non-stop flight to Orlando. After we got off the plane we had to catch a bus to our hotel. Not just any bus, the Disney's Magical Express! I have always wanted to ride one of these buses. The bus was much like any other similiar bus. Fabric seats, tv screen welcoming us to Disney's Magical Express, and of course a friendly driver. It was a short trip to the hotel, but also a check on the bucket list.

Hotel.. I mean Resort

We have been quite fortunate to have traveled to Disney World as a family mutliple times. This is definitely a memorable visit because of the AMAZING accomodations! We have always stayed in one of this cheaper resorts. There are 4 tiers of Disney resorts: Value, Moderate, Deluxe and Deluxe Villas. The resort we got upgraded to is considered a Deluxe Villa. Not the most expensive place to stay, but definitely not something we could afford at regular price. The resort was beautiful! There were animals roaming outside of the resort building, like Giraffes and Zebra. The inside was just as amazing as the outside. The amount of detail that Disney puts into their resorts is outstanding!

Itinerary

Normally on a Disney vacation our family goes all out. We get up early, get to the parks early, stay at the parks all day, go to bed late and wake up to do it all again. Our vacations are not very "vacation" like. This time we changed things up a bit. We decided to only visit 2 out of the 4 parks and gave ourselves a relaxation day and a beach day.
The day we arrived we went to the beach. It was late afternoon before we got to the beach so perfect time to enjoy a beautiful sunset. We played in the water and in the sand for a while. We headed down the beach to visit a local restaurant we had found. We got to enjoy a delicious fresh seafood meal beach side during the sunset.
The next day we went to Animal Kingdom. We got up early and made it to the park about an hour after they opened. The one ride we wanted to get on was The Flight of Passage in Avatar's Pandora area of the park. When we got in line for the ride there was a 3.5 hour wait. As crazy as it may seem to some people, we waited in that line. For over 3 hours we slowly walked in line behind hundreds of other people to ride a full immersive ride that takes less than 5 minutes to complete. The day was spent in a lazy walk site seeing the park and just being in the moment.
Our next day was spent at Epcot walking around the World Showcase. We love to visit Disney during Epcot's Food and Wine Festival. There are so many foods and drinks to try. The World Showcase area features several areas around a lake. The areas built for guests to enjoy are split up by different countries of the world. The beautiful architecture in each of the areas showcases the countries in such unique ways. During the Food and Wine Festival there is more food available and more countries showcased. This day was also a lazy day filled with food and laughter.
Our final day in Disney was spent in the pool at our resort and in Disney Springs. The pool at the resort was amazing. There was a large area to swim, slides that went through beautiful nature features and a play area for younger kids. It was such a nice change to finally have ample time to relax in the pool on vacation. Later we went to Disney Springs, which is an open shopping area. There are many stores to shop at, places to get meals and of course snacks. We enjoyed walking around the area, shopping and then finding a place to eat. We ate dinner at a cute Irish Pub, with talented employees Irish step dancing and playing live music, called Raglan Road. Another great day in Disney indeed.
